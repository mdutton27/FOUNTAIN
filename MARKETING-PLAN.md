## Part 3: All-of-Government (AoG) Panel Strategy

### 3.1 Understanding AoG Panels

#### What Are AoG Panels?
All-of-Government contracts are procurement agreements established by New Zealand Government Procurement (NZGP) that allow government agencies to purchase services from pre-approved suppliers without running individual procurement processes.

#### Relevant Panels for UX/Research

1. **Digital Services Panel**
   - Category: Discovery, User Research, Design
   - Administered by: DIA (Department of Internal Affairs)
   - Status: Check current panel status

2. **Consultancy Services Panel**
   - Categories: Business Advisory, Strategy
   - Administered by: MBIE
   - Broader scope, includes research and advisory

3. **ICT Services Panel**
   - Categories may include UX/UI design
   - Check current categories

### 3.2 Panel Application Process

#### Pre-Application Checklist
- [ ] Register on GETS (Government Electronic Tenders Service)
- [ ] Obtain NZBN (New Zealand Business Number)
- [ ] Register for GST (if turnover > $60,000)
- [ ] Obtain Public Liability Insurance ($1-2M minimum)
- [ ] Obtain Professional Indemnity Insurance ($1-2M minimum)
- [ ] Set up compliant invoicing systems
- [ ] Prepare company financials (2-3 years if available)
- [ ] Document health and safety policy
- [ ] Prepare references from previous clients

#### Application Components

**1. Company Information**
- Legal entity structure
- Company history and background
- Ownership and governance
- Financial stability evidence

**2. Capability Statement**
- Service descriptions
- Methodology documentation
- Tools and technologies used
- Quality assurance processes

**3. Experience & Track Record**
- Relevant project case studies (3-5)
- Client references (2-3)
- Team CVs and qualifications
- Similar government experience

**4. Pricing**
- Day rates by role/seniority
- Fixed price options
- Travel and expenses policy
- Rate card format

**5. Compliance**
- Health and Safety policy
- Privacy and security approach
- Subcontracting policy
- Sustainability/social procurement

### 3.3 Building Government Credentials

#### Before You Apply
1. **Subcontract first**
   - Partner with existing panel suppliers
   - Gain government project experience
   - Build references and case studies
   - Understand procurement processes

2. **Target smaller engagements**
   - Direct engagements under procurement thresholds (<$100K often)
   - Local government contracts
   - DHBs/Health NZ
   - Crown Research Institutes
   - Universities

3. **Build relationships**
   - Attend government digital events
   - Connect with digital leaders on LinkedIn
   - Participate in Digital Government Partnership
   - Engage with Service Innovation Lab alumni

#### Key Contacts
- **Digital.govt.nz** - Digital government standards and guidance
- **NZGP (Marketplace)** - Procurement and panels
- **GCDO Network** - Government Chief Digital Officers
- **GOVIS** - Government ICT community

### 3.4 Winning Government Work

#### Understanding the Buyer
Government clients value:
- **Risk mitigation** - Proven track record, references
- **Compliance** - Meeting all requirements exactly
- **Value for money** - Not cheapest, but best value
- **Capability fit** - Right skills for the job
- **Cultural fit** - Understanding of public sector context

#### Proposal Best Practices
1. Answer every question directly
2. Provide specific, relevant examples
3. Demonstrate understanding of their context
4. Show how you'll mitigate risks
5. Be clear on pricing with no surprises
6. Include relevant certifications and credentials

#### Government UX Standards
Familiarise yourself with:
- NZ Digital Service Standard
- Web Accessibility Standard (WCAG 2.1 AA)
- Privacy Act 2020 requirements
- Official Information Act considerations
- Te Reo Māori and cultural considerations

### 3.5 Pricing for Government

#### Market Rates (2025 indicative)
| Role | Daily Rate Range |
|------|------------------|
| Junior Researcher | $800-1,000 |
| Intermediate Researcher | $1,000-1,400 |
| Senior Researcher | $1,400-1,800 |

#### Pricing Strategy
- Position at market rate or slightly below to win initial work
- Build track record, then adjust rates
- Consider fixed-price for defined deliverables
- Factor in procurement overhead (timesheets, reporting)

### 3.6 Panel Application Timeline

#### Preparation Phase (3-6 months before)
- [ ] Research current panels and upcoming renewals
- [ ] Identify gaps in your capability/documentation
- [ ] Complete subcontracting projects for experience
- [ ] Gather case studies and references
- [ ] Prepare all compliance documentation

#### Application Phase (1-2 months)
- [ ] Monitor GETS for panel opportunities
- [ ] Download and analyse RFP documents
- [ ] Prepare comprehensive response
- [ ] Have response reviewed by someone with government experience
- [ ] Submit before deadline (allow buffer time)

#### Post-Application
- [ ] Respond promptly to clarification requests
- [ ] Prepare for potential interview/presentation
- [ ] If unsuccessful, request feedback
- [ ] Reapply at next opportunity
